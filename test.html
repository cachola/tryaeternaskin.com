Battle Of The Buds: How NeoPodz Stack Up Against Other Wireless Earbuds

By David Williams, writer for Gadget Guru

My infatuation with crisp, well-balanced, vibrant sound is a somewhat new obsession. For most of my life I was content with my average mediocre headphones. 


But one moment can reshape the way you hear the world. For me, it happened listening to a song I had heard a thousand times, but this time… with quality headphones at an audio store. 


It was like hearing sound with brand new ears. After experiencing music this way, I couldn't go back. 


But spending over $200 on headphones is madness to me . So I’ve been on a mission ever since to find the best headphones for the most reasonable price. 


So where do I start?


I compared each to see how the different pods stack up in terms of ease of use, comfort, music quality, and battery life. I’ve done an in depth assessment of sound quality for all the buds I reviewed. Hours were spent with each pair, listening to a variety of music including pop, rock, jazz, edm and classical in environments with different levels of outside noise. 


One of the main problems I noticed is that a lot of earpods have  a rattling or crackling bass at high volume or when connecting to another device. The boosted sound quality of NeoPodz deserves a special mention for it’s superb bass. Even complex songs retain a sense of distance between instruments so proceedings never become too muddied. It's an impressive showing. The sound is full-bodied, and the presentation is nicely open and spacious, giving each instrument room to work.

But it's their NeoBass silicone covers that's led them to surpass their competitors.  

You can get their  “NeoBass” noise cancelling silicone covers for only $12.99. These NeoBass covers transform the NeoPods experience with amplified bass, enhanced noise cancellation, and additional comfort, while acting as a protective layer to keep these pods in top notch shape. 

One of the biggest hassles I’ve dealt with when using wireless pods is them dying halfway through a flight, movie, or conference call. In addition to comfort and sound quality, the battery life (with Bluetooth) is  second to none. 

An impressive 30 hours, along with the supplied charging case to charge on the go. This is enough battery to last even the longest flight commuting across the world, giving them the title of #1 Travel & Leisure headphones for globetrotters in 2019.

Neopodz are a very viable alternative to the AirPods if you're on a tighter budget or just fancy saving some money.

This isn't the “cheapest” pair of AirPods alternatives, for someone who doesn't care about sound quality, there are cheap options at any rite aid, or walgreens. But Neopodz are the most pleasant option for high quality sound at a low price bracket.

Additionally  the price difference is huge so this may naturally become the deciding factor. The Neopodz provide great value for money since they only cost around $59.99, whereas the AirPods at $160 are a little overpriced for what they offer. In the end it comes down to the features you need and what price you’re willing to pay.
